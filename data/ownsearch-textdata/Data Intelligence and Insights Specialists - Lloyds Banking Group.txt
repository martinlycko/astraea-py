JOB TITLE: Data Intelligence and Insights Specialist
SALARY: £42,120 - £50,000
LOCATION(S): Birmingham
HOURS: Full-time
WORKING PATTERN: Our work style is hybrid, which involves spending at least two days per week, or 40% of our time, at our Birmingham office

 

About this opportunity

Are you passionate about turning data into meaningful stories? Do you thrive in an environment where analytics and creativity intersect? We're excited to announce a brand-new position within our Financial Crime & Compliance Technologies (FCCT) Screening Development team! This is an outstanding opportunity for a Data Intelligence and Insights Specialist to step into a role with unparalleled autonomy and the freedom to innovate.

 

Why Lloyds Banking Group

We're on an exciting journey and there couldn't be a better time to join us. The investments we're making in our people, data, and technology are leading to innovative projects, fresh possibilities, and countless new ways for our people to work, learn, and thrive.

 

What You'll Do:

Introduce the use of advanced analytics to drive strategic decisions.

Build compelling narratives through data visualisation using tools like PowerBI and Tableau.

Support the development of new technologies into the team.

Operate in the fast-paced and complex environment of sanctions compliance including UK, EU, and international sanctions.

Manage data with precision, ensuring integrity and accuracy.

Solve challenging problems and identify business opportunities with your excellent problem-solving skills.

Communicate complex technical results to a broad audience and collaborate effectively across various teams.

Demonstrate outstanding time management skills, handling projects independently from inception to completion.

 

What You'll Need:

 

Proven experience in a data analytics role where your ability to interpret complex datasets has brought actionable insights to life.

Demonstrable capability and experience in the use of data visualisation tools (such as PowerBI and Tableau)

Strong communication and collaboration skills, with the ability to engage with stakeholders at all levels.

The ability to work under pressure and meet tight deadlines without compromising quality.

An understanding of Economic Crime Prevention processes would be preferable but not essential.
